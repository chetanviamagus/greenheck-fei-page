Understanding Fan Energy Index (FEI)

FEI is a true “wire-to-air” metric that rates the overall energy consumption of a complete fan system.

Video

FEI is a ratio that allows you to accurately compare energy consumption between similar fan systems.

FEI is a tool that helps you select the best fan system for your application.

FEI is a mandate: minimum FEI values are mandated by codes.

What is FEI?

FEI
=
Baseline Electrical Input Power
Actual Electrical Input Power

FEI is a wire-to-air metric that represents true electrical power consumption by including motor, belt and bearing (if the fan is belt-driven), and fan aerodynamic losses at an application duty point.

The FEI metric is a powerful engineering tool for fan selection. Read our application guide to learn how it is calculated, what it says about fan speed and sound, and how to use it during submittal review.

BEYOND COMPLIANCE: APPLYING FEI IN FAN SYSTEM SELECTION

Select a fan type based on the application, duty point, and space constraints. Once you have determined the best fan type, use FEI to optimize the energy consumption.

ADVANTAGES OF USING FEI IN FAN SYSTEM SELECTION:

Higher FEI values equate to lower energy consumption, operating costs, and carbon emissions.

The only way to tell which fan system uses the least energy is to look at the FEI value.

Higher FEI fans have a forgiving selection point.

Selecting a fan with a higher FEI will give you a selection that is more central in the fan cure, giving you more room to speed the fan up if you need more air during test and balance.

Higher FEI fans are usually quieter.

FEI may also correlate to better sound performance. As an example, if comparing two sidewall fans at different sizes, the larger fan operates at a slower speed where it is more efficient and has a higher FEI value. Because it is operating at a slower speed, it is also quieter.

Higher FEI usually means you have selected a better fan for your application.

During submittal review, a small difference in FEI could mean a significant difference between comparable fans. An FEI difference can indicate that fan controls have been left out or the motor type is incorrect. If you compromise on FEI, there will be tradeoffs.
